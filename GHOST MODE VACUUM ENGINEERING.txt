PART 1: THE CONCEPT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Empty space is not empty. The quantum vacuum contains fluctuating
electromagnetic fields — virtual photons popping in and out of
existence. In free space, these fluctuations are uniform and
inert. But inside a tiny cavity — like a carbon nanotube — the
cavity walls reshape the vacuum. The fluctuation patterns become
structured, directional, and strong enough to push atoms around.

These structured vacuum patterns are called "ghost modes."

A Bose-Einstein Condensate (BEC) — a million atoms merged into
one quantum wave — placed inside a carbon nanotube feels the
ghost modes pulling it toward the wall. A superconducting (SC)
magnetic trap holds it in the center. The competition between
the ghost pull and the magnetic hold creates a measurable,
tunable, engineerable vacuum-matter coupling.

The result: a tabletop device that measures the energy of
"empty" space and connects to the vacuum physics of neutron
stars, the early universe, and the fundamental structure
of nothing.

PART 2: PHYSICS BACKGROUND

2.1 BOSE-EINSTEIN CONDENSATE (BEC)
──────────────────────────────────

A BEC forms when atoms are cooled to near absolute zero. At
that temperature, all atoms occupy the same quantum ground
state. They stop acting like individual particles and start
acting like one big quantum wave.

Key properties:
  - Temperature: ~50 nanokelvin (50 billionths of a degree)
  - Density: ~10^24 atoms per cubic meter
  - All atoms described by single wavefunction psi(r)
  - Atoms interact via s-wave scattering (contact force)
  - Coherence length: ~1 micrometer

Why a BEC? Because all atoms respond to ghost modes in
phase. A thermal gas of 10,000 atoms would give 10,000
random signals. A BEC gives one collective signal amplified
by 10,000.


2.2 CARBON NANOTUBE (CNT)
──────────────────────────

A cylinder of graphite, one atom thick, rolled into a tube.

Key properties:
  - Wall thickness: 0.34 nm (single layer graphene)
  - Diameter: 50-500 nm (our range of interest)
  - Length: 100 nm to 10 micrometers
  - Young's modulus: ~1 TPa (stronger than steel)
  - Interior is naturally ultrahigh vacuum
  - Can be open-ended (atoms enter from ends)
  - Can be fabricated on silicon or sapphire substrates

Why a CNT? Because the walls are atomically thin and
perfectly smooth. The electromagnetic boundary conditions
are clean. No roughness, no contamination, no defects
(at the level that matters for ghost modes).


2.3 ELECTROMAGNETIC GHOST MODES
────────────────────────────────

The electromagnetic field inside a cylindrical cavity has
discrete modes determined by the cavity geometry. These modes
are the cavity's version of the quantum vacuum.

Properties:
  - Evanescent: decay exponentially from wall into interior
  - Reach: 10-100 nm (depends on tube radius and wall)
  - Strength: depends on wall dielectric function
  - Spectrum: set by tube geometry (radius, length, wall)
  - Temperature: zero (quantum vacuum, not thermal)

The ghost mode interaction potential on an atom at distance
d from the wall:

  V_ghost = -V0 * exp(-d / lambda_ghost)

where:
  V0 = ghost mode depth (energy scale)
  lambda_ghost = evanescent reach (length scale)
  d = distance from atom to wall

These two parameters — V0 and lambda_ghost — are what the
experiment measures. They encode the electromagnetic vacuum
structure inside the CNT.


2.4 CASIMIR-POLDER INTERACTION
──────────────────────────────

Separate from ghost modes, the CNT wall exerts a direct
atom-surface force:

  V_CP = -C3 / d^3

For Rb-87 near carbon: C3 is a known constant.

This is cut off at d = 2 nm (sub-nm distances are
unphysical for continuum models). It is included in the
solver but is not the primary effect.


2.5 SUPERCONDUCTING MAGNETS
────────────────────────────

Magnets made from superconducting wire. Below their critical
temperature, they conduct electricity with zero resistance,
producing perfectly stable magnetic fields.

Properties:
  - Zero resistance → zero field noise
  - Field stability: <1 part per million
  - Can produce 1-20 Tesla routinely
  - Integrated with atom chip (thin film wires)
  - Operating temperature: 4 K (liquid helium)
    or 10 mK (dilution refrigerator)

Why SC magnets? Because the BEC is incredibly sensitive to
magnetic field noise. A normal magnet would heat the BEC in
milliseconds. An SC magnet keeps it cold for hours.

3.1 THE TUG-OF-WAR
──────────────────

Three forces on each atom in the BEC:

  1. SC TRAP (pulls to center)
     F_trap = m * omega^2 * r
     Set by SC magnet current. Tunable.

  2. GHOST MODES (pull to wall)
     F_ghost = (V0/lambda) * exp(-d/lambda)
     Set by CNT geometry. Engineerable.

  3. MEAN-FIELD REPULSION (pushes atoms apart at high density)
     F_mf = g * gradient(n)
     Set by scattering length. Fixed by species choice.

The BEC sits where these forces balance. The balance position
directly measures the ghost mode force.


3.2 THE CROSSOVER
──────────────────

The tug-of-war crossover occurs when the trap potential at
the wall equals the ghost mode depth:

  V_trap(R) = V0
  (1/2) * m * omega^2 * R^2 = V0

  f_crossover = (1/2pi) * sqrt(2 * V0 / (m * R^2))

For Rb-87, R = 100 nm, V0 = 50 neV:
  f_crossover = 530 kHz

Below 530 kHz: ghost modes win. BEC near wall.
Above 530 kHz: trap wins. BEC near center.
At 530 kHz: tug-of-war. BEC at intermediate position.

PART 4: KEY EQUATIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

4.1 GROSS-PITAEVSKII EQUATION (radial)
───────────────────────────────────────

The BEC wavefunction psi(r) satisfies:

  mu * psi = [-hbar^2/(2m) * laplacian + V_trap + V_ghost + V_CP + g*|psi|^2] * psi

where:
  mu = chemical potential (eigenvalue)
  m = atom mass
  V_trap = (1/2) * m * omega^2 * r^2
  V_ghost = -V0 * exp(-(R-r)/lambda)
  V_CP = -C3/(R-r)^3  (cut off at 2 nm)
  g = 4*pi*hbar^2*a_s/m


4.2 CROSSOVER FREQUENCY
────────────────────────

  f_cross = (1/(2*pi)) * sqrt(2*V0/(m*R^2))


4.3 FORCE AT EQUILIBRIUM
─────────────────────────

  F_trap = m * omega^2 * r_eq
  F_ghost = (V0/lambda) * exp(-(R - r_eq)/lambda)


4.4 MEAN-FIELD COUPLING
────────────────────────

  g = 4*pi*hbar^2*a_s/m

For Rb-87: g = 5.13e-51 J*m^3


4.5 OSCILLATOR LENGTH
──────────────────────

  a_ho = sqrt(hbar/(m*omega))


4.6 HEALING LENGTH
───────────────────

  xi = hbar / sqrt(2*m*g*n_peak)


4.7 BEC LIFETIME (from SC field noise)
───────────────────────────────────────

  dT/dt = (mu_B * delta_B)^2 / (hbar^2 * omega) * T
  tau = T / (dT/dt)


4.8 GHOST-BEC OVERLAP
──────────────────────

  Overlap = integral(n(r)*G(r)*r*dr) / integral(n(r)*r*dr)

where G(r) = exp(-(R-r)/lambda) is the ghost field.

PART 6: EXPERIMENTAL PROCEDURE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

6.1 EQUIPMENT NEEDED
────────────────────

  - Dilution refrigerator (base T = 10 mK)
  - Superconducting atom chip with wire pattern
  - SC bias coils (Helmholtz + anti-Helmholtz)
  - Current supply (programmable, low noise)
  - CNT array on atom chip substrate
  - MOT laser system (780 nm for Rb)
  - RF synthesizer (0.1 - 100 MHz)
  - Absorption imaging optics + CCD camera
  - Vacuum system (< 10^-11 mbar)
  - Data acquisition computer


6.2 ASSEMBLY
────────────

Step 1: Fabricate CNT array on atom chip
  - CVD growth of aligned CNTs (R = 100 nm, L = 500 nm)
  - Integrate with SC wire pattern (Nb or YBCO)
  - Characterize by SEM/TEM

Step 2: Install atom chip in vacuum chamber
  - UHV: < 10^-11 mbar
  - Connect to dilution refrigerator
  - Wire SC magnets to current supply
  - Install RF coils and imaging optics

Step 3: Create BEC
  - MOT loading (Rb-87, 780 nm cooling)
  - Sub-Doppler cooling to 10 microkelvin
  - Transfer to SC magnetic trap
  - Evaporative cooling to 50 nK
  - Verify BEC by time-of-flight imaging


6.3 MEASUREMENT SEQUENCE
─────────────────────────

Step 4: Position BEC near CNT array
  - Translate atom chip (piezo stage)
  - BEC within 1 micrometer of CNT opening
  - Load atoms into CNT interior

Step 5: Set trap frequency to 530 kHz (crossover)
  - Adjust SC magnet current
  - Verify frequency by parametric excitation
  - Measure mu by RF spectroscopy
  - Expected: mu = 78.8 neV (vs 118.3 neV without CNT)

Step 6: Frequency sweep
  - Sweep trap frequency: 100 kHz to 5 MHz
  - At each frequency:
    a. Wait 1 s for equilibrium
    b. RF spectroscopy pulse (10 ms)
    c. Absorption image
    d. Extract mu from RF spectrum
  - 20 frequency points
  - Total time: <1 minute

Step 7: Plot mu vs f
  - Expected: step function at 530 kHz
  - Below 530 kHz: mu = 43 neV (ghost-dominated)
  - Above 530 kHz: mu = 118 neV (trap-dominated)
  - Step height: 75 neV (the ghost mode signal)

Step 8: Control experiments
  a. Remove CNT (translate chip away)
     mu returns to 118.3 neV at all frequencies
  b. Use different CNT arrays (different R)
     Different crossover frequency = different V0
  c. Use different species (Na, K, Li)
     Different shifts confirm atomic physics


6.4 DATA ANALYSIS
──────────────────

Step 9: Extract ghost mode parameters
  a. Crossover frequency -> V0 = (1/2)*m*omega^2*R^2
  b. Step width -> lambda_ghost
  c. Step height -> coupling strength
  d. Compare to theory

7.1 WHAT IS POSITRONIUM
───────────────────────

Positronium (Ps) is an atom made of an electron and a
positron (antimatter electron). It is the lightest atom.

  Mass: 1.82e-30 kg (911 times lighter than hydrogen,
        79,000 times lighter than Rb-87)
  Lifetime: 142 ns (ortho-Ps, decays to 3 gamma rays)
  Detection: 511 keV gamma rays (self-announcing)


7.2 WHY Ps IS SPECIAL FOR GHOST MODES
──────────────────────────────────────

Reason 1: LIGHTNESS
  Ps is 79,000 times lighter than Rb-87.
  Ghost modes push it much harder relative to its mass.
  The ghost mode potential completely overwhelms any trap.
  No tug-of-war needed — ghost modes always win.

Reason 2: SELF-DETECTION
  When Ps annihilates, it produces 511 keV gamma rays.
  You don't need fancy detection equipment.
  Standard gamma ray detectors work.
  The timing of the gamma rays tells you the lifetime.

Reason 3: LIFETIME MODIFICATION
  Ps lifetime depends on the electromagnetic vacuum.
  Ghost modes change the vacuum.
  Therefore ghost modes change the Ps lifetime.

  This is the most direct ghost mode measurement possible.
  The Ps literally lives longer or shorter depending on
  the ghost modes around it.

7.4 THE Ps EXPERIMENT (no BEC needed)
──────────────────────────────────────

Equipment:
  - Positron beam (moderate energy, ~10 eV)
  - CNT array on substrate
  - Gamma ray detector (BaF2 or LaBr3 scintillator)

9.1 THE IMAGE CHARGE PROBLEM
─────────────────────────────

Charged particles near a conducting surface feel a classical
electrostatic force (image charge). This is 60-1000 times
stronger than the ghost mode force.

  At 30 nm from wall:
    Image charge force:  64 pN
    Lorentz force (10T): 1 pN
    Ghost mode force:    0.0002 pN

  Image charge dominates by far.
  Ghost modes are a small correction.


9.2 THE SOLUTION: SC vs NORMAL WALL
────────────────────────────────────

Superconducting walls SCREEN the image charge over the
London penetration depth lambda_L:

  Wall Material   lambda_L (nm)   Screening at 30nm
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Nb               40              63.5% (36.5% remaining)
  Pb               37              67.0%
  Nb3Sn            65              43.1%
  MgB2            140              21.1%
  YBCO            150              19.7% (80% screened!)

YBCO walls screen 80% of the image charge.


9.3 THE DIFFERENTIAL MEASUREMENT
─────────────────────────────────

  1. Measure cyclotron frequency in normal metal CNT
     omega_c(normal) = omega_c + Delta_omega_image

  2. Cool CNT below Tc (becomes superconductor)
     omega_c(SC) = omega_c + Delta_omega_image + Delta_omega_ghost

  3. Difference: Delta_omega_ghost = omega_c(SC) - omega_c(normal)

  The differential measurement CANCELS classical effects.
  What remains is PURELY the ghost mode contribution.

PART 10: TORUS EXTENSION
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

10.1 TUBE vs TORUS
──────────────────

  Tube (cylinder):
    - Open ends (atoms can escape)
    - Symmetric vacuum
    - Ghost modes uniform around circumference

  Torus (ring/donut):
    - Closed (no end effects)
    - Asymmetric vacuum (inner wall more curved)
    - Ghost modes stronger at inner wall
    - Persistent currents possible (quantized circulation)


10.2 THE ASYMMETRY
──────────────────

Inner wall of torus: tighter curvature -> denser EM modes
                     -> stronger ghost modes
Outer wall: gentler curvature -> weaker ghost modes

For minor radius a = 100 nm, major radius R0 = 1000 nm:
  Asymmetry delta = a/R0 = 0.1 (10%)
  V0_inner = V0 * (1 + delta) = V0 * 1.1
  V0_outer = V0 * (1 - delta) = V0 * 0.9

BEC shifts toward inner wall. The shift direction and
magnitude measure the curvature dependence of vacuum
fluctuations.


10.3 TORUS ADVANTAGES
─────────────────────

  1. No end effects (closed topology)
  2. Inner/outer asymmetry (new observable)
  3. Persistent currents (topological)
  4. Curvature dependence of ghost modes
  5. Particle circulation amplifies signal
     (thousands of ghost zone passes)


10.4 TORUS PARAMETERS
─────────────────────

  Minor radius a: 100 nm (tube cross-section)
  Major radius R0: 1000 nm (ring radius)
  Asymmetry delta: 0.1
  SC wall: YBCO coated
  Magnet: axial B field for circulation control
